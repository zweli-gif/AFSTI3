Africa Food Systems Transformation Initiative

Africa spends $100 billion importing food it can grow. Four corridors can change that.

$250–400M in coordinated investment across 4 corridors to unlock billions in intra-African trade and reach 150,000+ smallholder farmers

Five forces are converging. This is the window.

01

The Sovereignty Crisis

$62.8B

Sub-Saharan Africa's annual food import bill — accelerating past $100B continent-wide. The Ukraine shock revealed that African food security is hostage to shipping lanes, currencies, and conflicts it cannot control.

02

The Political Architecture

55 nations

All AU member states signed the Kampala Declaration in January 2025 — committing to triple intra-African agri-food trade by 2035. 49 countries have ratified the AfCFTA. For the first time, there is both a food mandate and a trade mechanism.

03

The Infrastructure

$10B+

Committed to the Lobito Corridor alone. Beitbridge modernised. TAZARA being rehabilitated. The Abidjan-Lagos highway carries $3B in annual trade. The corridors that were 'future tense' are becoming present tense.

04

Standalone Has Failed

17%

Zambia's soya crushing capacity utilisation. Twenty years of crop-by-crop investment cases produced islands of production without trade linkages. The constraint is not agronomic — it's systemic.

05

Capital Demands Integration

5 → 1

DFIs won't fund another standalone feasibility study. They want integrated cases where one investment de-risks another — where a feed mill makes aquaculture viable and a cold chain serves fish, tomato, and onions simultaneously.

Why integrated, not standalone

Each corridor is a system. One investment unlocks the next.

Four Integrated Investments

These are not standalone projects. Each investment de-risks the others.

IC-DL1

CIV Aquaculture Anchor Farm + Hatchery

$16–25M

Vertically integrated tilapia following the Yalelo model. Hatchery + anchor farm + processing to prove CIV can produce at commercial scale, then catalyse private replication.

$2.1BMarket sink
15,000+Outgrowers by Yr 5
Kill gate: Feed cost stays above $0.90/kg AND fingerling mortality exceeds 30% after 3 pilot cycles.
IC-DL2

ColdChainCo West Africa

$30–50M

Shared cold chain serving fish, tomato, onions, and mango across complementary seasons. 12-month utilisation makes unit economics work where single-commodity cold chains fail.

$3.5B+Multi-commodity sink
35,000+Connected producers
Kill gate: Annual utilisation below 60% after Year 3 — insufficient commodity diversity.
IC-DL3

Ghana Tomato Processing

$15–25M

Nigeria imports $360M in tomato paste annually, almost entirely from China. 40 years of failed attempts mean this must be structured differently — contract farming + reliable supply before processing.

$360MNigeria paste imports
8,000+Contract farmers
Kill gate: Ghana imposes tomato export ban OR Nigeria blocks paste from ECOWAS. Also: price test fails — $150/t above Chinese (conditional case).
IC-DL4

Sèmè-Kraké Border Facilitation

$10–15M

The Nigeria-Benin border is the corridor's biggest chokepoint. Digital green lanes, SPS harmonisation, and pre-clearance could cut crossing from 3–5 days to hours. This enables every other investment.

All tradeEnables corridor
IndirectReduces all costs
Kill gate: Nigeria refuses digital customs integration OR border remains weaponised for politics.
