First what exactly is a "trade corridor"?

Not a road. Not a commodity. A system where production basins connect to demand centres through investable infrastructure.

Production Basin
Yield gaps exist. Underutilised land and processing capacity. Aggregation potential untapped.

market access
Demand Sink
Import bill >$100M per commodity per corridor. Reliance on distant suppliers. Latent demand suppressed by high prices.

A corridor IS

Multi-country by definition a basin in Country A serving demand in Country B
Infrastructure-defined follows actual road, rail, port logistics routes
Multi-commodity enabled one corridor fix unlocks several value chains simultaneously
Investable trade friction can be reduced by targeted public and private capital
Measurable import bills, yield gaps, transit times, and cost breakdowns are all quantifiable

A corridor is NOT

Not just a road it includes policy, regulation, finance, and market systems
Not domestic import substitution a single country growing for itself is not a corridor play
Not commodity-first you don't start with "wheat is $18B" and then hunt for corridors
Not aspirational corridors must have existing or planned infrastructure to anchor investment
Not immune to politics political economy readiness determines which corridors can realistically trade

Three types of corridor opportunity AFSTI prioritises Type I & II

High Priority

Type I Corridor Trade

Country A produces and/or processes to supply Country B. Entirely intra-African. The core AFSTI play.

e.g. Zambia soya oil Angola/DRC
High Priority

Type II Corridor Value Creation

Country A produces, Country B processes, and supplies global markets. Value added stays on-continent.

e.g. CIV cocoa Ghana processing EU
Low Priority

Type III Global Export Only

Country A produces and processes to supply global markets with no corridor involvement.

e.g. Ethiopia coffee global (single origin)

The Sovereignty Crisis

$62.8B

Sub-Saharan Africa's annual food import bill accelerating past $100B continent-wide. The Ukraine shock revealed that African food security is hostage to shipping lanes, currencies, and conflicts it cannot control.

02

The Political Architecture

55 nations

All AU member states signed the Kampala Declaration in January 2025 committing to triple intra-African agri-food trade by 2035. 49 countries have ratified the AfCFTA. For the first time, there is both a food mandate and a trade mechanism.

The Infrastructure

$10B+

IC-DL5
DOMESTIC PLAY NOT CORRIDOR TRADE

Nigeria Integrated Rice Milling + Irrigated Outgrower Supply

$4080M

Irrigated outgrower supply + mechanised harvesting + milling upgrades in Nigerian rice basins. This is a domestic import substitution play, NOT a corridor trade case. Nigeria is a rice sink (imports 23Mt annually), not a basin it does not meet 60% of local demand and has no surplus for corridor export.

Why this is here despite failing the price test: The screening funnel flagged Nigerian rice as an E6 casualty (Asian rice $475/t vs Nigerian milled $795/t a 67% premium). However, the scale of Nigeria's food security exposure warrants inclusion as a domestic case. The interview data confirms: paddy production has halved to ~5Mt, 50% of mills are improperly set up, energy costs have doubled, and ~20Mt of installed milling capacity sits idle. The consumer tasters matrix also showed preference for imported rice on quality and consistency.
The honest case for investing anyway: Nigeria's rice demand is ~810Mt and growing at ~2.2 by 2050. Import dependence is a structural food security risk. Best-performing mills achieve 65% recovery and can approach cost parity at scale. The play is not about beating Asian rice on price it's about reducing sovereignty risk in a 200M+ person market. This requires the integrated "bundle": irrigated supply + harvest mechanisation + milling discipline + working capital.

The Corridor's Political Economy

Source: Political Economy Assessment, September 2025.

South Africa
AMBER-GREEN
PostureRegional anchor. Self-sufficient in feed.
Key riskHPAI disruptions. Anti-dumping politics on poultry.
OpportunityLargest SADC producer. 1.59Mt poultry. 14.5Mt maize. 1.9Mt soya.
SA's feed industry (13MMT) is the regional anchor what SA produces, the corridor can process and export.
Zimbabwe
AMBER-RED
PostureHigh potential, thin governance. Land reform legacy.
Key riskFX scarcity. GMB distortions. Power unreliability.
OpportunityIrrigated wheat potential. Private sector wheat/soya NOT subject to GMB.
Wheat and soya are privately led and freely tradeable the political risk that plagues maize does not apply here.
Mozambique
AMBER
PostureStructural demand sink. Governance improving.
Key riskInfrastructure deficit. Limited processing capacity.
Opportunity50kt poultry deficit. 80kt beef imports. 44% poultry self-sufficiency.
Mozambique's deficit is structural urbanisation and income growth are driving demand faster than domestic production can respond.
Malawi
AMBER-GREEN
PostureCooperative. Growing poultry sector.
Key riskMaize availability volatile. 22% post-harvest loss.
OpportunitySADC tariff-free. Soya cake from Zambia enables poultry growth.
Capital Foods is the largest buyer of both maize and soya in the country. Feed milling expanding to meet egg and chicken demand.
Botswana
AMBER-GREEN
PostureBeef exporter (EU). Stable governance.
Key riskBMC monopoly. Drought-stressed grazing. 100% feed import-dependent.
OpportunityEU market access for beef. Stable policy environment.
Botswana has the governance and the market access what it lacks is feed supply and processing scale beyond the BMC.

The Corridor's Political Economy

Source: Political Economy Assessment, September 2025.

Tanzania
AMBER
PostureSovereignty-first. Strong staples base.
Key riskExport bans, LGA fees, licensing discretion
Opportunity14Mt maize surplus. SSA #1 sunflower. $490M NFRA bond.
The government sees food security as food sovereignty being able to feed Tanzanians from Tanzanian soil. But the surplus is 4Mt and growing.
Kenya
AMBER-RED
PostureUrban affordability dominates. Corridor integrator.
Key riskImport duty waivers cyclical. County cesses. Electoral price management.
Opportunity$419M rice $100M maize $200M sugar imports. Active EAC trade.
Kenya acts more as a corridor integrator than a breadbasket Busia/Malaba and Moyale are the corridor's commercial arteries.
Ethiopia
AMBER-RED
PostureCommand economy on wheat. Border discretion.
Key riskFX scarcity. Episodic export windows. Chokepoints reduce trust.
Opportunity$442M SFO + $140M rice imports. SFO less politically controlled.
Ethiopia's wheat is under command economy logics, but sunflower oil and rice are not that is the corridor's entry point.
Uganda
AMBER-GREEN
PostureCooperative. Sugar exporter. Growing dairy.
Key riskLimited by processing scale. Landlocked logistics costs.
Opportunity450kt sugar production. Dairy to Kenya already trading. Stable policy.
Uganda is the corridor's most politically stable supplier Kakira and SCOUL sugar already flow to Kenya and DRC at scale.
