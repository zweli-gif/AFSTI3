Evidence Base

This is not theory. Corridor-led transformation has worked before.

1

Brazil Cerrado Santos

R&D adapted soya to tropical savanna. Intermodal logistics cut transport costs 30%. 115M+ tonnes/year, 253% growth.

2

GMS East-West Corridor

ADB investment cut transit from 8 days to 27 hours. Harmonised SPS across 6 countries. $17.7B invested.

3

US Grain Belt Gulf Ports

Barge + rail + grain elevator network. Warehouse receipt systems. Commodity exchanges. $45B+ annual ag exports.

4

IMT-GT EC1 (Thailand Malaysia)

Halal certification niche. AFTA barrier reduction. Inland port + rail at borders. $24.8B bilateral trade (2023).

Common Thread: Every success combined physical logistics with policy harmonisation, market linkages, and finance. Corridor investment is never just infrastructure.

Methodology

How we select basins and sinks the screening funnel

From $62B in African food imports 78 pairs 20 matches 912 bankable investment cases

1

Map Import Bill

78 pairs

Start with the $62B food import bill. Identify 78 commodity-country pairs where demand exceeds domestic supply. Threshold: >$100M per commodity per corridor.

2

Match Basin Sink

20 matches

Map production basins with surplus capacity to demand sinks. Must meet 60-80% of own demand or have clear plans within 5 years.

3

Existential Filters (E1E6)

Binary

Pass/fail gates. Any single FAIL = dead. Policy kill switches, production feasibility, infrastructure existence, demand validation.

4

Friction Scoring (F1F8) Prioritise

912 ICs

Continuous scoring on trade friction dimensions. All 4 food baskets, all 4 corridors represented. Final 912 DFI-ready investment cases.

Competitive Advantage

How corridors beat imports before you touch a seed

1

Working Capital Advantage

~$35/t

45-60 day ocean cycle vs 5-14 day corridor. At 22% interest, that's free money.

2

Transport Efficiency

~$120/t

800km by truck beats 12,000km by ocean despite Africa's higher per-km costs.

3

Tariff Preference

$65-385/t

COMESA/ECOWAS/EAC preferences vs MFN rates on non-African imports.

4

Agility Premium

$20-50/t

Respond to demand spikes in days, not months. Traders pay for certainty.

5

Border Friction Reduction

~$20/t

Every hour saved at Kasumbalesa is money. OSBP investments already reducing this.

Bottom Line: The question is not whether African farmers can produce as cheaply as Indonesia or Brazil. Given $50400/t of structural advantages, how much of the gap remains? That's what each investment case must close.

Strategic Framework

Three types of corridor opportunity AFSTI prioritises Type I & II

I

Intra-African Trade

Country A produces/processes to supply Country B. Entirely intra-African.

Example: Zambia soya oil Angola/DRC

HIGH PRIORITY
II

Value Addition

Country A produces, Country B processes global markets. Value stays on-continent.

Example: CIV cocoa Ghana processing EU

HIGH PRIORITY
III

Global Export Only

Country A global markets. No corridor involvement.

Example: Ethiopia coffee global

NOT PRIORITY
